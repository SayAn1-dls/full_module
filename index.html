Module 1 Probability & Random Variables

1.1 Axioms of Probability & Conditional Probability

Foundationв–ј

Probability measures how likely an event is. Before you can solve any stats problem, you must understand probability deeply.

Definition: Sample Space & Events

The sample space О© is the set of all possible outcomes. An event A is any subset of О©. The probability P(A) satisfies:

Kolmogorov's Three Axioms (The Rules of Probability)

  • Axiom 1 (Non-negativity): P(A) вүҘ 0 for any event A
  • Axiom 2 (Normalization): P(О©) = 1 (something always happens)
  • Axiom 3 (Additivity): If A вҲ© B = вҲ…, then P(A вҲӘ B) = P(A) + P(B)

From these 3 axioms, EVERY other probability rule is derived. They are not optional вҖ” they define what probability IS.

рҹ’Ў Why these axioms matter in exam

Exam questions often ask you to verify whether a given function is a valid probability distribution. Check: all probabilities вүҘ 0, and they sum to exactly 1.

Key Derived Results

  • P(Aб¶ң) = 1 вҲ’ P(A) (complement rule)
  • P(A вҲӘ B) = P(A) + P(B) вҲ’ P(A вҲ© B) (addition rule)
  • P(вҲ…) = 0
  • 0 вүӨ P(A) вүӨ 1
Definition: Conditional Probability

The probability of A given B has occurred: P(A|B) = P(A вҲ© B) / P(B), where P(B) > 0.

Think of conditional probability as "restricting" your sample space to B, then asking how much of it is A.

Multiplication Rule
P(A вҲ© B) = P(A|B) В· P(B) = P(B|A) В· P(A)

Independent Events

A and B are independent if P(A вҲ© B) = P(A) В· P(B), equivalently P(A|B) = P(A). Independence means knowing B tells you nothing about A.

вҡ пёҸ Common Exam Mistake

Mutually exclusive вү  independent. If A вҲ© B = вҲ… (mutually exclusive) and both have positive probability, then P(AвҲ©B) = 0 вү  P(A)В·P(B). So mutually exclusive events with positive probability are actually DEPENDENT!

1.2 Bayes' Theorem

High Weightageв–ј

Bayes' Theorem is one of the most powerful tools in probability. It lets you "reverse" conditional probability вҖ” find P(cause | effect) from P(effect | cause).

Bayes' Theorem
P(Aбөў | B) = P(B | Aбөў) В· P(Aбөў) / ОЈвұј P(B | Aвұј) В· P(Aвұј)

The denominator ОЈвұј P(B | Aвұј) В· P(Aвұј) = P(B) is called the Total Probability.

Terminology

Prior P(Aбөў): What you believed BEFORE seeing evidence B.
Likelihood P(B|Aбөў): How likely evidence B is under hypothesis Aбөў.
Posterior P(Aбөў|B): Updated belief AFTER seeing evidence B.

рҹ’Ў Step-by-step approach for Bayes problems

1) Identify all mutually exclusive & exhaustive events AвӮҒ, AвӮӮ, ... (the "hypotheses")
2) Find P(Aбөў) вҖ” prior probabilities
3) Find P(B|Aбөў) вҖ” likelihood for each hypothesis
4) Compute P(B) = ОЈP(B|Aбөў)В·P(Aбөў)
5) Apply Bayes formula

1.5 Binomial Distribution B(n, p)
